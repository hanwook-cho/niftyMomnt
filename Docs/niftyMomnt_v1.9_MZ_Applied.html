Capture Hub — changes
1
Preset name — black weight
was 700 · 1.5px spacing now 900 · 0.5px · 17px. Same word, completely different energy. Reads as a camera's physical selector dial, not a lifestyle editorial label.
2
Shutter — matte physical
was glassy white sphere now radial-gradient(#F2F2EC → #C0C0B9) with inset shadow. Less Apple-icon, more real camera button. The inset highlights give it physical weight.
3
Preset peek swatches
5 color dots in Zone C. Active is larger. Signals a collection at a glance — users discover the swipe without needing to find the long-press gesture.
Post-Capture — changes
4
Location chip — moved here
was live viewfinder chrome now top of post-capture overlay. Zero viewfinder impact. Surfaces at the exact moment users are choosing a vibe tag — contextually useful.
5
Sticker chips — tilt + bounce
Each chip has a unique tilt −2.5° / +1.8° / −1.2° / +2.2°, staggered spring entrance 0 / 58 / 116 / 174ms, and varied border-radius. Feels like slapping labels, not filling a form. Huji/BeReal energy.
6
Share pill — purple, not amber
was neutral glass now brand purple tint. Visually signals "sharing" vs "shooting" — a different mode. Also reduces amber overload across the chrome.
✦ The key shift
The post-capture screen is now intentionally more expressive than the capture screen. Gen Z wants the shooting experience invisible — and the moment of reflection to have personality. The sticker chips + location context + purple pill create a distinct "what just happened" beat.

Roll complete — new state
7
"roll's done. / we'll develop it."
When 0 shots remain: warm amber flood fills the viewfinder, all film strip frames glow amber, and bold personality copy replaces the scene. The develop ✦ CTA routes to the Film archive. Shutter shows 🔒. Gen Z screenshots and shares this state — it's the film metaphor's payoff moment.
8
Film strip: all frames glow amber
All 9 frames filled and glowing. Counter reads 0 left in amber. Visual completion satisfies the gamification loop — the strip is a reward, not just a counter.
Film archive — terminology
9
"Film" replaces "Journal"
was "Journal" / "Moments" now "Film". The tab icon becomes 🎞. "Journal" reads mindfulness-app; "Film" completes the analog camera metaphor that runs through the entire experience. Also shorter — fits the tab bar better.
10
▶ icon replaces "Make Reel" button
was "Make Reel" text pill now a circular ▶ in the card's accent color. "Reel" is peak Instagram-2021 language. The icon is universal, smaller, and lets the card breathe. Color matches the preset that was active during that moment.
✦ The film metaphor, completed
v1.9 runs one continuous metaphor: shoot → fill the strip → roll's done → develop → Film archive. Every state has character. This is what makes an app feel designed by someone who cares rather than assembled from a component library — and it's exactly what MZ shares on their finsta.
