Q01
Does it follow iOS 26 UI?
Needs Update
v1.9 uses flat blur(20px) + rgba(0,0,0,.36) — the old iOS 17/18 frosted glass. iOS 26 Liquid Glass requires saturate(200%) brightness(1.12) + inner-glow highlights + floating pill tab bar. Updated across all zones in v2.0.
Q02
Is the colour scheme trendy?
Partially
Amber is the hero colour and stays — strong, distinctive, film-authentic. Cyan (#00E5CC) is a circa-2018 fintech teal — dated for a film aesthetic app. Replaced with soft lavender (#C4B5FD), trending in Gen Z apps (Notion, Arc, Locket). Brand purple deepened to #6B4EFF.
Q03
Top bar icons too small?
Needs Fix
Yes. Emoji glyphs (⏱⊙⚡) at 14px inside 30×38px pills are below Apple's 44pt tap-target guideline and look non-native. In v2.0: inline SVG SF Symbol-style icons (timer, AF-lock, flash, overflow) in 40×40px round pills with Liquid Glass treatment. Active flash pill amber-tinted.
Q04
Film screen layout good?
Major Revamp
The light-mode (#F5F4F0) card list is a jarring contrast switch from the dark capture experience, and feels generic iOS. Popular film apps (Dispo, Darkroom) use dark editorial aesthetics. Revamped to dark #0F0D0B, larger heroes, horizontal thumbnail strip, floating iOS 26 pill tab bar.
Q05
Moment Detail screen OK?
Missing → Added
Not included in v1.9 at all — a significant gap. Added as a new screen: full-bleed photo with preset's film grade visible, location chip, floating tilted sticker vibes, swipe-navigation dots, and a glass bottom sheet with roll info, vibe tags, "Fix this shot" and Share actions.

Capture Hub · Film Archive — changes applied
1
SVG icons replace emoji — 40×40pt pills
before Emoji (⏱⊙⚡) at 14px in 30×38px pills — below Apple's 44pt tap-target guideline, render inconsistently across OS, non-native look. after Inline SVG glyphs in 40×40 round pills: clock face (timer), corner-frame with centre dot (AF lock), filled lightning bolt (flash — amber-tinted when active), three horizontal dots (more). Matches Halide, ProCamera icon vocabulary.
2
iOS 26 Liquid Glass on all zones
before backdrop-filter: blur(20px) + flat rgba(0,0,0,.36). after blur(28px) saturate(200%) brightness(1.12) + inner top/bottom edge glows. Zone A, C, D all updated. Matches UIVisualEffectView .systemUltraThinMaterialDark with the new Liquid Glass refraction that iOS 26 introduced system-wide in Fall 2025.
3
Film archive: dark editorial, not light mode
before White #F5F4F0 background — jarring brightness jump from dark camera, looks like a generic iOS Notes list. after Dark #0F0D0B editorial background — consistent with the film/photography aesthetic (Dispo, Darkroom, Halide all use dark archival UIs). Warm near-black reinforces the "developing your roll" metaphor. Preset accent colour carries into each card's hero strip.
4
Floating pill tab bar — iOS 26 signature pattern
before Full-width white bar pinned to bottom edge — the iOS 17/18 default, being phased out in iOS 26. after Floating glass pill (border-radius: 29px) with Liquid Glass material hovering 16pt above the screen edge. This is iOS 26's defining tab bar pattern (seen in Messages, Photos, Music beta builds). Active tab uses lavender accent dot — replaces the old cyan.
5
Thumbnail strip — photo-forward cards
before Card showed only text metadata below hero. after Horizontal thumbnail strip exposes 4–5 actual shots from the roll. Users immediately see what's in each roll without opening it. Overflow "+N" chip shows remaining count. Matches how BeReal Memories and Dispo Rolls present content. Hero height increased to 130pt to support the richer layout.

Moment Detail — design rationale
1
Full-bleed photo — preset grade visible
Photo fills screen edge-to-edge with the preset's vibe tint and film grain applied — the exact look the user captured. Light leak persists from the capture session's overlay. Swipe left/right navigates shots within the roll. Shot position is shown via the pill-and-dot indicator at photo bottom. VSCO and Halide set this expectation for Gen Z: the photo is everything, chrome steps back.
2
iOS 26 glass bottom sheet
Liquid Glass material slides up over the photo — saturate(200%) brightness(1.12) + inset 0 1px 0 rgba(255,255,255,.08). Contains: roll name, shot number, timestamp, vibe tag row, and action buttons. The drag handle affords swipe-to-dismiss to reveal the full photo. Bottom padding uses env(safe-area-inset-bottom) for home indicator coverage on all iPhone 15–16 sizes.
3
Floating sticker vibes — personal, not metadata
Vibe tags from the post-capture flow appear as tilted sticker chips over the photo — same spring-bounce animation as the post-capture prompt, same tilt angles. This makes the emotional tags feel genuinely owned and placed (like stickers on a physical print) rather than cold system metadata. Tap a sticker to edit the tag. Two stickers are shown; a third is visible on the edge to imply more.
4
Zone A: back → roll → share
Back button (left) returns to Film Archive with a slide-right transition. Centre shows roll name + moment title in 900-weight — orients users without a separate header. Share button (right, lavender) triggers UIActivityViewController — identical to the Quick Share Pill from post-capture, so the same share path is consistent across contexts. All three buttons are 40×40pt.
5
"Fix this shot" — §5.3 Fix Modal entry
Prominent action alongside Share — opens §5.3 Fix Modal (exposure, crop, preset swap). Gen Z expects quick in-app touch-ups (VSCO, Adobe Lightroom Mobile established this norm). The circle-check icon signals non-destructive refinement, not a full re-edit. The action is placed left-of-share so the eye lands on Fix before Share — nudging towards quality before distribution.
PRD + Spec update needed for this screen
§5.2 Moment Detail in the UI/UX Spec needs: (1) Zone A layout with back/title/share spec, (2) floating sticker vibe rendering rules (position, tilt, tap-to-edit behaviour), (3) swipe-navigation gesture + pagination indicator. PRD §3.5 should cross-reference this detail view from the Film Archive card tap interaction.

iOS 26 Liquid Glass — What Changed
iOS 26 (shipped Fall 2025) replaced the flat frosted glass of iOS 17/18 with Liquid Glass — a vibrantly translucent, refractive material that responds to content underneath. It appears across the OS: tab bars, navigation bars, control centres, and sheets. Not adopting it makes an app look one generation behind.
